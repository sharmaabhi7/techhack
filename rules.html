Rules and Regulation

Complete set of event rules, regulations, and participation guidelines

TECHHACK 2025 Hackathon Registration Guidelines and Rules

1. Team Composition:

  • Each team should consist of a maximum of 5 members including a team leader and minimum 2 Members.
  • The team leader will be responsible for the coordination and communication throughout the event.

2. Single Problem Statement:

  • Each team must work on only one problem statement of their choice. Multiple problem statements may not be pursued by the same team.
  • One student may only be part of one team and cannot participate in multiple teams.

3. Team Member Eligibility:

  • All team members must belong to the same School/College/University.
  • No inter-institutional teams are allowed. However, members from different branches of the same School/College/University are encouraged to form a team.
  • Open to UG, PG, and Research Students.
  • Students from Computer Science, Engineering, IT, and related disciplines are encouraged.

4. Post-Regional Round Team Changes:

  • After the first round, no changes to the team members are allowed.
  • Team composition must remain unchanged for the duration of the hackathon.

5. Accurate Registration:

  • All details entered during registration on the google form must be accurate and correct. Any incorrect or misleading information may result in the disqualification of the team.

6. Guideline Compliance:

  • Teams must strictly follow all guidelines and instructions throughout the hackathon.
  • Failure to comply with the rules may result in penalties or disqualification.

7. Team Member Identification:

  • The names of team members must be provided as per the official records (School/College/University ID).
  • Any discrepancies in member identification may lead to team elimination.

8. Code of Conduct:

  • Strict adherence to ethical coding practices.
  • Plagiarism or any form of misconduct will result in disqualification.

9. Decision-Making:

Decision taking authority is the jury and the jurisdiction to Rai University committee only.

Tie-Breaking Guidelines for Hackathon

Extra Evaluation Criteria

  • If two or more teams have equal scores, judges can consider additional parameters such as:
    • Innovation & Originality – Uniqueness of the idea.
    • Feasibility & Scalability – Practical implementation and future potential.
    • Impact & Relevance – Usefulness for society/industry.
    • Presentation & Communication – Clarity in explaining the solution.

Jury Deliberation Round

Judges may conduct an additional Q&A round with tied teams to assess their depth of understanding and teamwork.

Prototype Demonstration

A short live demo or stress test can be organized to evaluate functionality under real conditions.

Bonus Points

Extra points may be awarded for aspects like:

  • Proper documentation & code quality.
  • Open-source compliance.
  • Ethical use of technology.

Shared Prize (Last Resort)

If no fair distinction can be made, teams may share the prize with equal recognition (though this should be avoided if possible).
